**Strong**: The adjective strong is used in a number of different ways to describe people. When you say that someone is strong, you usually mean that they have powerful muscles and the ability to lift or carry heavy objects. Claudia was young, strong, and healthy.

The little boy has grown into a tall, strong man. A strong personality is someone who is very confident and not easily influenced by others. Alan possesses such a personality with notable leadership qualities that are highly beneficial for this club. A strong believer in something is someone convinced that it is very good or desirable. The Secretary of State is a strong believer in parental involvement in classrooms. To support a person or organization enthusiastically, you are a strong supporter of the NHS. Be careful! If someone smokes or drinks excessively, do not describe them as a 'strong smoker' or a 'strong drinker'. Instead, say that they are a heavy smoker or a heavy drinker.
